@cnumber Thanks for raising this, it is a recurring issue in Kaggle competitions. Not kaggle fault, rather the complexity of IP laws and regulations.
Disclaimer: We cannot provide legal advice. Contact someone trained on US IP laws if you need advice.
With the above disclaimer, here is our thought.
The purpose of this competition is not to train models that would compete with commercial models. NVIDIA will not reuse the adapters produced by participants. After all, we could train models ourselves if that was our intent, given we have the data generator.
The goal of the competition is to see how people manage to fine tune Nemotron model. The pipelines are more interesting to us than the outcome.
Given the above, we won't bother if people distill models for this competition provided that the only limit to model output use is to forbid training competing models. In particular, distilling Gemini Flash 2.0 outputs looks fine for this competition. For any other use please check with someone trained in IP law.
