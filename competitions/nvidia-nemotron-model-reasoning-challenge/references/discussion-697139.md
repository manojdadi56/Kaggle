The Rise of "Brain Rot" Submissions in Nemotron Challenge (Updated Daily)
Kaggle Competition Integrity: The Nemotron "Wall of Shame"
I’ve noticed a significant uptick in low-effort, plagiarized, or "word-farmed" notebooks being published in this competition. Specifically, users are:
Plagiarizing Baselines: Simply copying existing high-LB (Leaderboard) notebooks and changing the title or adding random comments.
Word Farming: Adding large blocks of AI-generated or irrelevant text to notebooks to bypass spam filters or game the "hotness" algorithm.
"Random" Updates: Re-publishing the same notebook daily with minor, non-functional changes just to stay at the top of the "Recently Updated" list.

🧐 Masterclasses in "Fake it Till You Make It"
🛡️ GlyphMatics💯🖖
The "GlyphMatics" notebook is the ultimate "fake it till you make it" artifact—a masterclass in dressing up a skeleton in a tuxedo and calling it a revolution. It’s essentially a high-schooler’s HTML project wrapped around a single line of standard linear algebra that’s been public knowledge since the 70s.
By branding a basic SVD truncation as "Residual-Matched PairFold RowGuard Transport," the author is doing more "transport" of hot air than actual weight matrices. It’s the coding equivalent of a car salesman putting a racing stripe on a lawnmower; you can add all the emojis and "energy gain caps" you want, but it's still just a plagiarized script suffocating under fifty layers of AI-generated Markdown fluff.
📐 Geometry of Intelligence: SVD, LoRA & Art Compression
"Geometry of Intelligence" is a title doing incredibly heavy lifting for a notebook that is essentially just a "Find and Replace" job on a basic SVD script. It tries to frame the mechanical act of trimming a matrix as high-concept "Art Compression."
In reality, it’s just the digital equivalent of taking a high-res photo, saving it as a low-quality JPEG, and then writing a 10-page thesis on the "Abstract Minimalism of Pixelation." It’s 1% utility, 99% aesthetic, and 100% designed to trick people into clicking the upvote button.
🛠️ The "I Can’t Actually Code" Suite
🧊 Nemotron Local CV
This notebook is a sprawling monument to over-engineering. It implements "caching" functions that essentially just read files into RAM—something the OS already does—and a regex-heavy extraction function that looks like it was written by someone trying to explain math to a toaster. It’s a "utility script" that provides zero utility; just a glorified wrapper for vLLM.
🎣 End-to-End Finetuning: The "False Advertising" Special
The title claims "End-to-End Finetuning," but contains zero training logic. It is a total bait-and-switch. Instead of finetuning, it’s just another copy-paste of the same SVD compression script, hidden under a "monkey-patch" nobody asked for.
📦 NVIDIA_CONSTRAINT_AI_COMP: The "Digital Hoarder"
This notebook imports 24 different data sources, model versions v1 through v14, only to select one and zip it. The description uses buzzwords like "heterogeneous model configurations" to describe what is actually just a script that copies a folder into a .zip file. It’s pulling in AI Mathematical Olympiad (AIMO) utilities for no reason—it's not "scalable design," it's just messy housekeeping.
🎭 The "Copy of a Copy"
🔢 Tinker w/ SVD & Rank 16
The only "innovation" here is changing a single integer: FORCED_FUSED_RANK = 16. By dropping the rank to 16, the author is literally throwing away half the model's remaining intelligence just to satisfy a self-imposed constraint that doesn't actually help performance.

📉 Why This Hurts the Competition
Clutter: It makes it impossible for serious participants to find genuine research on prompting, synthetic data, or fine-tuning.
Reputation: It devalues the "Open Contribution Awards" meant for meaningful advances in reasoning performance.
Resource Waste: These notebooks run unnecessary compute sessions just to produce a submission file that already exists.
📢 Call to Action
Report: If a notebook is a direct copy without attribution, use the "Report" flag for plagiarism.
Filter Quality: Focus on notebooks that actually implement original research or functional improvements.
