Midpoint Leaderboard Clarification + Open Contribution Award Question
Hi organizers. Two quick questions that I think will help the community plan the next two weeks.
1 - Midpoint Prize: Which Leaderboard?
The rules state the Open Progress Prize is:
"Awarded to the team with the highest leaderboard score as of the Midpoint Cut-off Date: April 9, 2026."
Could you clarify whether this refers to the public leaderboard or the private leaderboard score at the cutoff? This distinction matters quite a bit for training strategy, optimizing against the public 50% versus building for generalization against the full test set are meaningfully different approaches.
2 - Open Contribution Awards: Methodology Scope
I'm particularly interested in the Best Data/Synthetic Data Method award. My public score is currently modest, but that's because I've been investing heavily in custom data generation rather than optimizing exclusively for the known categories. After studying the competition training set, I've developed original reasoning puzzle categories designed to stress-test generalization beyond the provided distribution. The idea is to train a model that reasons about structure rather than memorizing templates.
Here's one example… a polysemy disambiguation puzzle:
The following paragraph contains multiple words with several meanings: 'bank', 'lead'. How many TOTAL distinct meanings are used across all these words?
"mechanical pencil lead snapped, meanwhile you can bank on me and the bank approved the loan and asked to lead the team then the lead was too short then the bank was muddy and steep"
My question: since the Open Contribution Awards require a top 10% finish on the final leaderboard, is the intent that custom data contributions should primarily improve final leaderboard performance? Or is there value in the methodology itself even if the custom categories are designed for generalization beyond the scored distribution?
Appreciate the clarification! Excited about this competition. Im so ready!
